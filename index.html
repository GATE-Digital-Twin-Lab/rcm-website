Reliable Computational Modelling

Setting the standard in reliable digital twins

The reliability of any model is measured by the trust decision-makers can invest in its pronouncements. The RCM group develops approaches and methods to objectively assess and improve the reliability of data-based, physics-based and mixed models.

Reliable modelling in practice

Research areas

From method to real-world use — our research lines in multiphysical modelling, uncertainty quantification, scientific machine learning and reliable language modelling, taken across industry and the built environment.

Reliable industrial modelling

Structural and seismic design of industrial facilities, and structural analysis for the automotive industry — engineering decisions backed by quantified confidence in the underlying simulations.

Covers the full modelling and simulation cycle — geometry preparation, mesh development, boundary and initial conditions, multiphysics coupling, visualization and postprocessing. Applications span the built environment and indoor studies, ground and aerial vehicle simulation, and industrial production. Current use cases include CFD for cultural-heritage preservation, air-pollution dispersion, and certification of passenger aircraft.

Reliable urban environmental modelling

How wind, heat and pollutants interact in cities — using CFD to test interventions that improve air quality and pedestrian comfort. Our work includes the ERIES-ValUr wind-tunnel campaign at TU Eindhoven and pedestrian-level wind studies over Sofia.

Uncertainty quantification

UQ sits at the border of fundamental and applied research: uncertainty characterization and propagation through complex numerical models, automatic computation verification, and generalized uncertainty calculi. It is applied to design under uncertainty, risk assessment of complex systems, and model validation. Use cases: aircraft certification, aerodynamic design in industry, air-pollution model validation, and machine-learning reliability.

Scientific machine learning

Reliability of physics-informed and deep-learning models, applied to surrogate modelling, simulation acceleration, and digital-twin fusion. Current use cases cover aerospace structural modelling and urban analysis.

Reliable language modelling

Equipping large language models with statistically rigorous guarantees of validity represents a critical step toward the reliable integration of LLMs into high-stakes applications. As these complex systems become integral components of an increasing number of workflows and pipelines, errors introduced at any stage can propagate and compromise the integrity of downstream processes. Rendering language models auditable through uncertainty quantification methods, such as conformal prediction, brings the field closer to principled, trustworthy collaboration between humans and LLMs.

Digital Twin Laboratory

Academic–industrial co-innovation at the heart of the group — where research-grade methods meet real engineering problems. We partner across technology-readiness levels, from joint research-excellence programs to knowledge-transfer partnerships and research-based services.

Through the unCertify project we are building a UQ framework for simulation-enabled aircraft certification — reducing reliance on costly flight testing while preserving an equivalent level of safety. It is validated on the AIAA CbS challenge.

Selected projects

Projects

National and international projects where our reliable-modelling methods are put to work.

Digital Twin Cities Centre (DTCC)

Hosted at Chalmers University and one of 21 competence centres funded by Sweden's innovation agency VINNOVA, DTCC works to make city-scale digital twins the foundation for planning, designing, building and managing sustainable, smart and inclusive cities. Its research spans eight areas covering the full digital-twin technology stack, with a strong focus on transferring knowledge to industry and urban administration.

Read more

AUDiT

AUDiT develops digital-twin models that explicitly account for imperfections and uncertainties in their surrounding environment and in the predictions that follow. The work brings together uncertainty and risk assessment, statistics, engineering and data science.

unCertify

unCertify develops automatic, maximally rigorous uncertainty-quantification methods and tools to enable design for certification, supporting aircraft certification by simulation. Reliable computational models replace a growing share of physical experiments, lowering costs and lead times while opening the market to a wider range of participants.

Smart Farm

Smart Farm applies computer vision to assess the health of plants directly from imagery. It turns ordinary visual data into early, actionable indicators of crop condition.
